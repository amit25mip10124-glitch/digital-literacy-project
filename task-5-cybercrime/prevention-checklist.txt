🔐 Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

✅ General Safety Tips

Use strong, unique passwords for every account (mix of letters, numbers, symbols).
Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.
Never click on suspicious links in emails, SMS, or social media messages.
Verify website URLs before entering personal information (look for https://).
Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities like banking or logging into important accounts.
Keep your devices updated with the latest software and security patches.
Install apps only from trusted sources like official app stores.
Do not share personal details (OTP, passwords, ID proofs) with unknown people online.

💳 UPI & Financial Safety (Important)

Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone — banks will never ask for it.
Always double-check the receiver’s UPI ID/name before sending money.
Avoid scanning QR codes from unknown or suspicious sources.
Do not accept random “collect requests” unless you trust the sender.
Use official banking apps only — avoid third-party or unknown payment apps.

📱 Social Media Safety

Keep your profiles private and control who can see your posts.
Do not accept friend requests from unknown people.
Avoid sharing your live location, phone number, or personal data publicly.
Think before posting — once online, content can be permanent.

🚨 What To Do If Something Goes Wrong

Immediately block the suspicious account/transaction.
Inform your bank if any financial fraud occurs.
Change your passwords for affected accounts.
Report the incident using:

🌐 National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
📞 Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (24×7)
💡 Smart Habit

If something feels urgent, threatening, or “too good to be true” — pause and verify before acting.
